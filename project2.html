Overview

Rockbuster Stealth is moving from DVD rentals to a global streaming platform. I used SQL to query the company’s database and Tableau to visualize findings that identify high-value markets, revenue-driving genres, and priority customer segments for the launch strategy.

Role: Data Analyst • Tools: SQL (PostgreSQL) and Tableau • Timeline: ~2 weeks

Goal

Support Rockbuster’s streaming rollout with data-driven guidance on where to launch first, what content to emphasize, and which customers to retain and grow.

Process

Data & Queries

  • Joined core tables (payments, rentals, films, categories, customers, locations) to build revenue and usage views.
  • Applied CTEs/subqueries and window functions to rank countries, cities, films, and customers by revenue.
  • Aggregated by category to find top-grossing genres and to inform catalog priorities.

Analysis & Visualization

  • Built Tableau/Excel visuals for stakeholder consumption (genre revenue, top countries, customer value tiers).
  • Profiled high-value customers and identified loyalty opportunities.

Key Insights

  • Genres: Sports and Sci-Fi are the top revenue drivers; Animation, Drama, and Comedy follow closely. :contentReference[oaicite:2]{index=2}
  • Markets: India, China, and the U.S. lead in total revenue and are strong candidates for phased rollout. :contentReference[oaicite:3]{index=3}
  • Customers: High-value segments rent more frequently and respond to targeted incentives. :contentReference[oaicite:4]{index=4}

Outcome

Delivered a prioritized market list, genre focus for catalog acquisition, and loyalty tactics for top customers to improve early streaming ROI. :contentReference[oaicite:5]{index=5}
