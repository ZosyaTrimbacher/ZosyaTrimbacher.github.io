Overview

For Instacart, I analyzed purchase behavior and built customer segments to improve retention and targeting. The work covers data cleaning and feature engineering, behavioral profiling, and visualization of spend, timing, and product preferences.

Role: Data Analyst • Tools: Python, Excel, Tableau • Timeline: ~2 weeks

Goal

Enable more effective marketing and loyalty programs by understanding when customers buy, what they buy, and which segments drive value.

Process

Data Preparation

  • Imported, cleaned, and merged large Instacart datasets; handled duplicates, missing values, and mixed types. :contentReference[oaicite:6]{index=6}
  • Engineered features (loyalty flag, spend tiers, segment profiles) used throughout the analysis. :contentReference[oaicite:7]{index=7}

Behavioral Analysis

  • Identified peak spend times (early morning 4–5 a.m. and evening 8–10 p.m.) and higher weekend pricing. :contentReference[oaicite:8]{index=8}
  • Profiled product demand—Produce, Dairy & Eggs, Snacks among top categories. :contentReference[oaicite:9]{index=9}
  • Segmented customers (loyal, new, regular) and compared spend distributions. :contentReference[oaicite:10]{index=10}

Key Insights

  • Regular customers are the largest cohort; loyal customers contribute disproportionately to value. :contentReference[oaicite:11]{index=11}
  • Spending spikes in the early morning and late evening—prime windows for promotion. :contentReference[oaicite:12]{index=12}
  • Top departments suggest where to focus category promotions and bundles. :contentReference[oaicite:13]{index=13}

Outcome

Recommendations covered timing-based promotions, loyalty upgrades for regular customers, segment-specific bundles, and category-focused marketing to lift retention and LTV. :contentReference[oaicite:14]{index=14}
