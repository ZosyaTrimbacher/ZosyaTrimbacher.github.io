Influenza Staffing — Case Study (Overview)

About Projects Resume Contact Impressum

Overview

Estimate staffing needs during the U.S. influenza season and provide a clear, data-driven plan for allocating nurses, doctors, and PAs across states. I cleaned and merged CDC influenza deaths with U.S. Census demographics, analyzed seasonal and age-group patterns, and built a stakeholder-friendly Tableau storyboard.
Role: Data Analyst • Tools: Tableau, Excel • Timeline: ~2 weeks

Process

Research & Analysis

  • Merged CDC influenza deaths with Census population by state; handled missing values and consistency checks.
  • Explored mortality by age group and state; confirmed elderly risk (65+) with a one-tailed t-test.
  • Checked correlation between elderly share and flu deaths to inform prioritization.

Implementation & Visualization

  • Tiered states into low / medium / high staffing need using vulnerability indicators.
  • Built spatial (state) and temporal (monthly) views to reveal where and when demand spikes.
  • Packaged insights in a concise Tableau storyboard for non-technical stakeholders.

Results

  • Peak demand consistently falls in Dec–Feb (often highest in January); activity rises from October and drops after March.
  • States with large senior populations show higher risk, guiding who should receive extra coverage first.
  • Large states like CA, NY, and TX show sustained burden across seasons, informing baseline allocation.

Outcome & Next Steps

Clear seasonal windows and a state tiering framework to minimize under/over-staffing during surges. Limitation: deaths-only data underestimates total clinical load; next iteration would add hospitalizations or ILI visits.
